Lesson 2: Propositional Connectives and Truth Tables

Connectives

The connectives are:

Negation

Given any statement p, another statement, called the negation of p, can be formed by writing “It is false....” before p or, if possible, by inserting in p the word “not”.
Symbolically,
¬ p
Denotes the negation of p (read “not p”)

P ¬P
True False
False True
Unary Operator

A unary operator takes a single operand(P/statement)

Conjunction

Any two statements can be combined by the word “and” to form a compound statement called the conjunction of the original statements.
Symbolically,
p & q
Denotes the conjunction of the statements p and q, read “p and q”.

P Q P & Q
True True True
True False False
False True False
False False False

Disjunction

Symbolically,

Exercise 1:

Example

Let P be the proposition the house is white.

Let Q be the proposition the garage is green.

Then


¬P the house is not white
P & Q the house is white and the garage is green
P | Q the house is white or the garage is green
P => Q if the house is white then the garage is green
P <=> Q the house is white if and only if the garage is green
